Obscure History: Miyamoto Musashi

Firstly: Who the Hell is he?

If you have an interest in Feudal Japanese History, you may have heard the name Miyamoto Musashi. And if you are not interested... I don't really care, I'm going to tell you a story anyways. So, just who in the hell is this guy? Well the short version is that he is a Bushi, (which is another term for Samurai), Ronin, Sumi-E artist, author, and Japans Most successful duelist. His crowning achievement was his school of combat which literally translates too "The Way of Two Heavens as One." This school was written about in a series of scrolls which were compiled into a book, called the Book of Five Rings.

Early Life

The Boring Part

Musashi is believed to be born during the Sengoku Era in the year 1584, in the Harima Prefecture. And thats it. Thats all the Noteworthy information according to Musashi himself, as Outlined in the Books of Five Rings. His life gets interesting around the age of 13, when he fights in his first duel.

Dueling Career

The Cool (Albiet Bloody) part

Musashi says he fought in over 60 duels, almost half of them were to the death. He never lost. His fighting style became Legendary, as he was known for duel wielding swords, and, despite being in a culture where honor and decorum in combat were valued, Musashi often times played dirty, pressing his advantages by taking cheap shots at opponents. His most famous duels were with his rival, a man named Saski Kojiro. Their rivalry was the Fuedal Japan equivalent of Muhammed Ali and George Foreman.

Art, and the Book of Five Rings

His last Endeavours

Musashi was raised in the Zazen, (Japanese phrase for Zen Buddhism), practice. The ideas of Zen Buddhism, and Bushido combined to create a message of mastery of the self. This is through intelligence, spirituality, art, poetry, and combat. Musashi spent the last few years of his life mastering art and philosophy. He was a prolific artist, mastering the Sumi-E style.
